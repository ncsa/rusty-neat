The extreme-purity collapse is normal-starvation — and pinning the normal fixes it

Two purity sweeps on chr22 — different callers and variant classes, one shared mechanism. A fixed budget starves the matched normal at high purity; a healthy normal does not.

SV recall · Manta · fixed 60× budget (§3.7) SNV recall · Mutect2 · normal pinned 30× (re-run)

Under a fixed budget the matched normal shrinks with purity and recall collapses at the extreme (SV recall → 0.17 at purity 0.9, normal ~6×); with the normal pinned at 30× recall instead holds ~0.97 right through 0.9. The two lines are not the same measurement — each is the clean test within its own variant class — but they make one point: the collapse is normal-starvation common to any tumor/normal caller (Mutect2 or Manta loses the germline reference it subtracts), not rneat's variant generation. With a healthy normal, SNV recall even rises with purity and plateaus at the detection ceiling (≥0.7); it is marginally lower at purity 0.3 (0.87) only because the pinned-normal design leaves less tumor depth there. Fix tracked as #315 (decouple matched-normal depth from purity). Source: ACCESS report §3.7 + §4 purity re-run.
