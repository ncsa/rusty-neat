The purity collapse was normal-starvation, not rneat

Mutect2 somatic SNV recall on rneat tumor/normal pairs (chr22), sweeping tumor purity two ways: splitting a fixed sequencing budget vs holding the matched normal at a fixed 30×.

Fixed budget — matched normal shrinks (§3.9) Matched normal pinned at 30×

Pinning the matched normal at 30× eliminates the collapse: recall holds at ~0.97 through purity 0.9, where the fixed-budget sweep cratered to 0.17. Under a fixed budget, purity 0.9 starves the normal to ~6×, so Mutect2 loses the germline reference it subtracts — an experiment-design artifact, not an rneat or purity-handling defect. With the normal healthy, recall instead rises with purity and plateaus at the detection ceiling (≥0.7). It is marginally lower at purity 0.3 (0.87) only because pinning the normal leaves less tumor depth there (13× vs 18×). Recommendation: hold normal coverage fixed when sweeping purity. Source: ACCESS report §3.9 + §4 purity re-run.
